Meet the Scholars

Get to know the SLU students who became Gilman Scholars one of which I was lucky to meet during my time in studying in SLU's Kenya Semester Program.

Ruby Bradford

2025 Gilman Scholar | SLU Global Studies & History

"The Gilman Scholarship provided me with the financial support to enjoy my semester in Kenya without the worry of what it would cost me. My experience in Kenya was shaped by my incredible peers, professors, and directors who supported me academically and personally throughout the semester. I had the opportunity to spend a semester immersing myself in experienced-based learning, short term travel components, site visits, classes, and a short term internship in human rights which is a field I hope to pursue. KSP provoked intentional critical thinking about every topic we studied, as well as provided students with unique experiences across the country including visiting various farms, sectors, and learning about the socioeconomic and political issues within each region. My time abroad wouldn't have been the same without Gilman, which allowed me to thrive and learn rather than be stuck worrying about financial constraints. It allowed me to pursue every opportunity I was excited for without the stress of it was the right decision to go abroad. I would reccommend KSP to every student, regardless of background, as it has something valuable to offer for anyone that goes."

Henry Julca

2025 Gilman Scholar | SLU Computer Science

"I cant further express how grateful I am to have been selected as a Gilman Scholar and been able to experience the SLU Kenya Semester Program. This scholarship has not only alleviated the financial burden associated with studying abroad but has also opened doors to a world of opportunities and experiences that I would not have had otherwise. Immersing myself with Kenya's vast and rich culture while learning about the challenges locals face was an eye opening experience and one I recommend for anybody studying any major. Knowing my background wasn't going to help much, I took everything head on and it paid off in multiple ways fostering new connections and sparking conversations about climate and governance. I left Kenya ready to take on the world of adulting with memeories and experiences that shaped me into the person I am today. Being a Gilman Scholar meant I was able to connect with others like myself and harbor new connections while simultaneously not having to worry much about the financial hardships one might experience studying abroad. I strongly encourage anyone pursuing studying abroad to apply for the Gilman A. Benjamin Scholarship as it supported me in my journey every step of the way."
